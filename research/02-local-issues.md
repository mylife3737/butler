## Issue 1 — Property insurance affordability (HEAT: **HIGH**)

- **The problem.** Florida homeowners' insurance is the highest in the nation. Carriers have pulled out of the state or gone insolvent; Citizens (the state-backed insurer of last resort) has ballooned; post-Ian / Milton claim denials are widely reported. 2022's SB 2A reform removed one-way attorney fees, making it harder for homeowners to sue carriers. This is THE #1 kitchen-table issue in coastal SW Florida.
- **Data points:** ⚠️ UNVERIFIED — single-source at time of writing. Pull current figures from:
  - [Florida Office of Insurance Regulation](https://floir.com/)
  - [Insurance Information Institute — Florida homeowners rates](https://www.iii.org/)
  - Recent Tampa Bay Times / Herald-Tribune / Charlotte Sun reporting on Citizens rate filings.
- **Headlines to pull:** Sarasota Herald-Tribune and Charlotte Sun archive searches on "property insurance" / "Citizens" / "insurance rates" 2024-2026.
- **Campaign hook:** *"I've run calls into houses that flooded twice and got denied both times. My opponent is a commercial realtor — he votes with the insurance lobby while you pay the bill."*

## Issue 2 — Hurricane recovery (HEAT: **HIGH**)

- **The problem.** Charlotte County took a direct hit from Hurricane Ian (Sep 28, 2022). Milton (Oct 9, 2024) and Helene (Sep 2024) compounded damage across SW Florida. North Port and Port Charlotte have lingering unrepaired roofs, blue tarps, mobile-home park devastation, FEMA appeals stuck in queue, SBA loan denials, and elevation-cost mandates that made many homes economically unrepairable.
- **Data points:** ⚠️ UNVERIFIED — pull from:
  - [FEMA OpenFEMA DR-4673-FL (Ian)](https://www.fema.gov/openfema-data-page/disaster-declarations-summaries-v2)
  - Charlotte County & Sarasota County damage assessment reports
  - USA Today Network "A Year After Ian" / "Two Years After Ian" coverage
- **Headlines to pull:** Charlotte Sun, NBC2, Fox 4 Fort Myers — searches for "Ian recovery", "Milton damage", "blue tarps", "mobile home park" in 2024-2026 archives.
- **Campaign hook:** *"I was running emergency calls the night Ian came ashore. Three storms later, my neighbors still have tarps on their roofs. Tallahassee moved on — I won't."*

## Issue 3 — Healthcare access + rural EMS (HEAT: **HIGH** — Cynthia's wheelhouse)

- **The problem.** HD 75 spans rural coastal communities with long EMS response times, aging population, and a hospital landscape in flux: Sarasota Memorial opened a Venice campus in 2021; HCA Englewood, Bayfront Port Charlotte (now HCA Florida Fawcett), and ShorePoint Punta Gorda have had ownership and service-line changes. Florida is one of 10 non-expansion states — closing the Medicaid coverage gap would pull ~1.4M Floridians into coverage, including thousands of HD 75 residents working in hospitality, retail, and home health.
- **Data points:** ⚠️ UNVERIFIED — pull from:
  - [KFF Medicaid Coverage Gap data](https://www.kff.org/)
  - Florida Hospital Association EMS response-time reports (if public)
  - [Florida Department of Health EMS statistics](https://www.floridahealth.gov/)
- **Campaign hook:** *"I've been the one in the back of the ambulance. I know which neighborhoods we get to in 6 minutes and which take 22. Your zip code shouldn't decide whether you live."*
- **Contrast with Nix:** Commercial realtor — no healthcare credential, no EMS / hospital / patient-facing experience. This is a pure-credibility axis where Cynthia wins on biography alone.

## Issue 4 — Overdevelopment / housing affordability (HEAT: **HIGH**)

- **The problem.** Wellen Park (North Port) and West Villages annexation disputes, Sarasota County 2050 comprehensive-plan fights, mobile-home park closures and rent spikes in Charlotte County, workforce-housing scarcity pricing out teachers / nurses / first responders, and short-term-rental (Airbnb/VRBO) conversion of owner-occupied homes.
- **Data points:** ⚠️ UNVERIFIED — pull from:
  - Sarasota County Planning Department + Charlotte County Community Development
  - [Shimberg Center — Florida housing data](http://www.shimberg.ufl.edu/)
  - Recent Herald-Tribune + Sun coverage of Wellen Park and 2050 plan amendments
- **Contrast with Nix:** He is a **commercial real-estate broker** AND former president of the Charlotte County Economic Development Partnership — a developer-facing trade group. This is his single largest optics vulnerability. Any vote he cast or casts on a density bonus, impact-fee reduction, or STR preemption is a direct "bought and paid for" attack.
- **Campaign hook:** *"He sells the land. He runs the developer group. Then he votes on the housing laws. You tell me who he's working for."*

## Issue 5 — Water quality / red tide / environment (HEAT: **MED-HIGH**)

- **The problem.** Red tide (Karenia brevis) blooms along Charlotte Harbor + Lemon Bay / Placida; nutrient runoff from agriculture (Peace River watershed) and stormwater; septic-to-sewer battles in unincorporated Charlotte and Sarasota; Piney Point fallout still in legal/regulatory tail; Lake Okeechobee discharges into the Caloosahatchee reaching SW coast.
- **Data points:** ⚠️ UNVERIFIED — pull from:
  - [Florida Fish and Wildlife Research Institute — Red Tide](https://myfwc.com/research/redtide/)
  - [Florida Department of Environmental Protection](https://floridadep.gov/)
  - Calusa Waterkeeper reports
- **Campaign hook:** *"Our beaches are why people move here. If we don't protect the water, we lose the jobs, the home values, the way of life — everything."*

## Issue 8 — Reproductive rights (HEAT: **MED** — persuasion caution)

- **The problem.** Florida's 6-week abortion ban (SB 300, effective May 1, 2024 after Amendment 4 failed the 60% threshold in Nov 2024). Cynthia's lived experience as a paramedic gives her unusual standing to discuss medical emergency / ectopic / miscarriage-management edge cases that broke under the ban.
- **Strategic note:** Amendment 4 won a majority (57%) in November 2024 but fell short of the 60% constitutional threshold. That tells you: HD 75 voters likely majority-pro-choice at the moment of truth, even while voting R for candidates. Frame as medical emergency / doctor-patient / grandmother's rights — NOT as "abortion rights," which loses by brand.
- **Data points:** ⚠️ UNVERIFIED county-level A4 splits — pull from the FL DOE 2024 general canvass.
