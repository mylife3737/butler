# Underserved-Constituency Outreach

Three voter constituencies Florida Democrats chronically under-invest in — and where a paramedic-nurse Democratic candidate in HD 75 has structural advantages. Each plank is designed to produce measurable impact with near-zero marginal dollar cost.

---

## Plank 1 — Spanish-language outreach (North Port + Port Charlotte Latino voters)

### The opportunity
Both North Port and Port Charlotte have a growing Latino population (⚠️ UNVERIFIED specific share; pull ACS 5-year estimates for the precincts in HD 75 — in prior cycles the share was somewhere between 8% and 15% of the electorate, varying precinct to precinct). Republican-oriented media saturation in Spanish is heavy in Miami-Dade; less heavy in Charlotte + Sarasota. That means bilingual content here faces less crowded channels.

### The ask of Cynthia
- Does Cynthia speak any Spanish? (INTAKE — verify; if yes, asset; if no, surrogate-driven.)
- Accept that the Latino vote in SW Florida is NOT monolithic; it includes Cuban-American families (historically R-lean), Puerto Rican voters (more D-available), Mexican-American communities, and recent Venezuelan and Nicaraguan arrivals. Message must be local ("tu comunidad," "tu familia") not pan-Latino.

### Tactics (all free or near-free)

1. **Bilingual surrogates.** Recruit 2-3 Spanish-speaking volunteers who can:
   - Record a 30-second Spanish-language video introducing Cynthia to their own network.
   - Appear at bilingual events (Port Charlotte festivals, Latino-owned businesses).
   - Translate canvass scripts for Spanish-dominant households.
   - Recruit source: local Catholic parishes, Latino-owned restaurants and service businesses, Venezuelan / Puerto Rican community groups, Telemundo / Univision Fort Myers viewers.
2. **Translated one-pager.** Full translation of Cynthia's bio one-pager + top 3 issues. Paid translation ~$50-$150 via a local bilingual volunteer or ProZ/community-vetted translator.
3. **Spanish-language social content.** 1-2 posts per week in Spanish once a surrogate volunteer is in place. Message variants:
   - Insurance: *"Mis primas de seguro se triplicaron. Las tuyas también. Voy a luchar por nosotros."*
   - Healthcare: *"Trabajé como paramédica durante 20 años. Sé por qué la ayuda tarda tanto en llegar. Eso va a cambiar."*
4. **Spanish-language press.** Pitch **Univision / Telemundo Fort Myers** for a Spanish-language interview once the campaign has a Spanish-speaking surrogate booked.
5. **Community events.** Identify 2-3 Latino community events in HD 75 (festivals, church fairs, quinceañeras network). Show up with palm cards + bilingual VBM request forms.

### Where not to go
- Do NOT lead with national immigration debates (even "defending immigrants" can cut against you here depending on community composition — some conservative Cuban voters punish it).
- Do NOT assume shared culture/language across Latino subgroups.
- Do NOT use "Latinx" in Spanish-language content. Use "la comunidad Latina" / "los Latinos" / specific national-origin terms where appropriate.

### KPIs
- 1 Spanish-speaking surrogate recruited by end of Sprint 2.
- 1 translated one-pager by end of Sprint 2.
- Spanish-language posts average ≥50% of English engagement rate by Sprint 4.
- 1 Spanish-language press interview by Sprint 4.

## Plank 2 — Faith-community outreach

### The opportunity
Two distinct faith-community tracks:

**Track A — Black churches (primarily Charlotte County):**
- Historically Democratic-voting; high-propensity voters once mobilized.
- Often under-visited by challengers (assumed support = no attention given).
- Black voter turnout in FL 2024 was down materially from 2020 — meaningful GOTV opportunity.

**Track B — Mainline/progressive white churches:**
- UCC, ELCA Lutheran, Episcopal, Unitarian Universalist, Reform Jewish congregations, some progressive Methodist congregations.
- Smaller membership but highly-engaged voters with donor / volunteer capacity.

### Tactics

1. **Pastoral outreach list.** Identify 5-10 target congregations across HD 75:
   - Port Charlotte: 1-2 historic Black churches (⚠️ names TBD; research via Charlotte County Black Heritage Society, local pastoral associations).
   - Punta Gorda / Englewood / Venice: mainline / Episcopal / UCC congregations.
   - Sarasota County side: multiple progressive congregations in Venice.
2. **Meeting request protocol.** Cynthia + her campaign manager meet with each pastor/rabbi/priest privately. Goals:
   - Introduce the candidate personally.
   - Understand the congregation's priorities.
   - Ask: would you be open to allowing a meet-and-greet in the community space (NOT during worship)?
   - Ask: would you be open to letting Cynthia speak to the congregation during announcement time?
   - Ask: would you be willing to distribute VBM request information to the congregation?
3. **Respect the boundaries.** Churches are not political platforms. Cynthia does NOT preach, does NOT endorse political positions from the pulpit, does NOT use church settings for direct asks.
4. **Sunday-afternoon events.** Host "meet Cynthia" events in community spaces adjacent to (not inside) congregations.
5. **Bible-literate surrogates.** Where Cynthia's own faith credentials aren't strong (INTAKE §4.4), recruit a surrogate congregation leader willing to vouch.
6. **Avoid:** prosperity-gospel framings that don't match Cynthia's actual voice; appearing at a church whose leadership has made politically divisive public statements; skipping Black churches (common strategic error).

### KPIs
- 3 pastoral meetings by end of Sprint 2.
- 1 Sunday-adjacent event by end of Sprint 3.
- Measurable VBM uptake from congregation outreach by end of Sprint 4.

## Plank 3 — Recovery community outreach

### The opportunity
Southwest Florida has substantial populations in recovery from opioid/alcohol addiction. The state's response to the opioid crisis has been mixed; paramedics are often the first point of contact for overdoses and crisis interventions.

Cynthia's paramedic background gives her unique credibility here that no other candidate in HD 75 will have. This is also a quietly large constituency — estimated 10-15% of American adults are in recovery or supporting someone in recovery.

### The ask of Cynthia
- Comfort level discussing the issue? (INTAKE §8 - if healthcare/opioid is ranked, this plank activates.)
- Personal experience? (optional; no pressure to disclose — but if present, adds power.)

### Tactics

1. **Narcan distribution events.** Partner with Charlotte County Health Department or a local addiction-treatment nonprofit to host 2-3 "get Narcan, get a voter registration form" events. Near-zero dollar cost; event sponsor provides the Narcan; Cynthia provides presence + narrative. High earned-media value.
2. **Recovery-community surrogates.** Cynthia's EMS colleagues almost certainly include people whose recovery work is a calling. Recruit 1-2 as surrogates.
3. **12-step program ethics.** Cynthia does NOT politic inside meetings — that's strictly off-limits. Recovery-community outreach happens at:
   - Adjacent coffee shops, community centers.
   - Partner organizations (recovery centers, sober-living facilities).
   - Public events with recovery themes (Recovery Month in September).
4. **Message framing.**
   - Medicaid expansion for opioid treatment access.
   - Rural EMS funding for overdose response.
   - Drug-court funding.
   - Treatment vs. incarceration framing.
5. **Sensitivity.** Do not center every event on recovery (alienates some families). Do not out any individual. Use "people in recovery and their families" language, not medicalized language.

### KPIs
- 1 Narcan-distribution event by end of Sprint 3.
- 1 recovery-community surrogate recruited.
- Recovery-community messaging integrated into 1-2 earned-media hits per sprint.

## Cross-plank principles

### Time allocation
- Each plank gets a named owner (a volunteer lead).
- Cynthia personally commits 2-4 hours/week to underserved outreach starting Sprint 2.
- No plank substitutes for the core door + phone + event program; these are additive.

### Measurement
All three planks feed into the voter-file tagging system — every contact from an underserved-outreach activity gets tagged in the CRM so we can measure turnout lift among touched populations on Election Day.

### Respect discipline
- Every plank requires humility: Cynthia is asking to be welcomed into communities, not claiming ownership.
- Every plank requires followup: underserved-community members notice when a candidate shows up once and never returns.
- Every plank requires local leadership: surrogates from the community are always more credible than the candidate.

### Anti-tokenism
Do NOT use underserved-community photos in fundraising emails to donors who are not themselves from those communities. Do NOT publish "look who I met with today" photos that imply endorsement when there is none. Do NOT conflate one community meeting with a coalition relationship.
