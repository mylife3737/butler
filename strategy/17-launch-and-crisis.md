# Launch Event & Crisis Comms SOP

## Part 1 — Launch event spec

### Goals
1. Produce a visual moment (photos, short video) that seeds the next month of content.
2. Convert 70-150 attendees into a mix of donors + volunteers + email subscribers.
3. Earn a same-day press pickup from Florida Politics + at least one local TV + one print outlet.
4. Publicly inaugurate Cynthia's voice in the district.

### Timing
- **Ideal:** Saturday after qualifying (week of June 13-20, 2026). Morning or early-afternoon to beat Florida heat.
- **Alternate:** if Cynthia's availability doesn't align, do it the following Saturday.

### Venue shortlist (free / cheap)

Rank by availability + political fit:
1. **Supporter's large home with yard** — $0, warmest feel, best for Mamdani-style "kitchen-table campaign" optics. Needs 75+ yard capacity, parking for 30+ cars.
2. **Union hall** (if endorsed) — $0 if endorsed; ~$100-$200 rental otherwise. Good for labor-focused framing.
3. **Public park with permit** — $25-$75 for permit. Weather-dependent. Good visually.
4. **Community center** (Port Charlotte, Punta Gorda, Englewood) — $50-$150 rental. Indoor, reliable.
5. **Restaurant with a back room** — $0 if they expect food orders. Less visual pop.
6. **Democratic club meeting** — piggyback a DEC or DWC meeting. Pros: pre-assembled audience. Cons: smaller, less media-friendly.

**Avoid:** the house of a wealthy donor (donor-class optics), a hotel ballroom (corporate), a purely digital / Zoom launch (misses the physical-presence value).

### Invite list tiers

- **Tier A (50-100):** Cynthia's personal network (INTAKE §4) + committed volunteers + donors + endorsers + DEC members.
- **Tier B (50-100):** email subscribers who opted in pre-launch + house-party connectors + friendly local Democrats + union members.
- **Tier C (open public):** social media invites + neighborhood word-of-mouth + DEC newsletter.

Build a Tier A list of 200 people; 30-40% attendance is normal.

## Part 2 — Rapid-response / crisis-comms SOP

**Principle.** Not every attack deserves a response. The wrong response amplifies the attack. The right response reframes it. Speed matters, but clarity matters more.

### The 24-hour decision tree

```
                Attack / negative story lands
                        |
                        ▼
            Within 1 hour: manager + comms assess
                        |
          ┌─────────────┼─────────────┐
          ▼             ▼             ▼
        IGNORE        RESPOND      ESCALATE
          |             |             |
          ▼             ▼             ▼
     Monitor       Hours 2-6       Hours 2-24
     only.         Statement       Full press
     Track         from Cynthia    push + media
     spread.       or surrogate.   outreach.
```

### Triage criteria

**IGNORE** if:
- Source is obscure (blog, small Facebook account).
- Claim is easily refuted but amplifying = more eyes on the claim.
- It's a hit piece in a closed R-only community that won't reach persuasion universe.

**RESPOND** if:
- A Tier 1 outlet ran it (Charlotte Sun, Herald-Tribune, a TV station).
- It's gaining traction on Facebook (>5K reach).
- Nix's official account posted it.
- Silence would be taken as admission.

**ESCALATE** if:
- National news picks up.
- It implies legal wrongdoing.
- It affects Cynthia's personal safety or family safety.
- It's a coordinated multi-outlet hit.

### The 5 likeliest attacks + pre-drafted counters

(See also `15-message-box.md §Q3.)

### Attack A: "She's inexperienced — has never legislated."
- **Cynthia's response (1 FB post + 1 paragraph for press):**
> "I've spent 20 years in rooms where people's lives are on the line. I've worked shifts while raising my kids. I've put myself through school. 'Experience' in Tallahassee has produced the insurance crisis, unrepaired hurricane damage, and a rural EMS system that's broken. The experience Tallahassee needs is what I've got."
- **Do NOT:** list legislative credentials we don't have.
- **Surrogate echo:** former colleague says "She ran IVs in ditches during Ian. She can read a bill."
- **Time:** 4 hours from attack.

### Attack B: "She was on welfare/government assistance."
- **This is already part of the core narrative — we win this one by owning it.**
- **Cynthia's response:**
> "Yes, I was. WIC and Pell paid for my nursing school while I was raising my kids. I paid that back in taxes many times over. My opponent voted to cut the programs that helped me become a paramedic — the same paramedic who later helped his constituents. I'd do it again."
- **Surrogate echo:** a pastor or neighbor frames it as "this is what the social compact looks like, working."
- **Time:** 2 hours.

### Attack C: "She's a radical Democrat."
- **Cynthia's response:**
> "I'm a paramedic. I'm a nurse. I'm a Florida Democrat — the kind that fights insurance companies on your behalf. Call me whatever you want; I've got a job to do."
- **Time:** 2 hours. Keep it short.

### Attack D: "She'll raise your taxes."
- **Cynthia's response:**
> "My opponent's voted to make your insurance premiums explode. That's a tax. I'll fight to lower the things you actually pay every month — starting with insurance."
- **Time:** 4 hours.

### Attack E: "[Personal history weaponization from §14 self-audit]"
- **Pre-drafted per item in `14-cynthia-self-audit.md`.**
- **Time:** 1 hour (if this lands, stop everything).

### Attack F: "She supports [culture-war issue] that'd destroy Florida."
- **Cynthia's response (firm, brief, pivot):**
> "I trust women. I trust doctors. I trust teachers. My opponent wants the state to make medical and educational decisions for you. That's not freedom. That's his record. Now can we talk about your insurance?"
- **Time:** 4 hours.

### Universal response rules

- **Never lie.** Not even a small one.
- **Never trash-talk Nix personally** (family, appearance, religion). Attack his record, donors, conflicts.
- **Always name the source** (reporter, outlet, Facebook account) when responding — it helps readers evaluate credibility.
- **Default medium = Facebook post** (widest reach in HD 75). Follow with email if the story is big.
- **Press statements are signed by Cynthia** or by comms lead in writing. Nothing anonymous.
- **Keep responses short.** One paragraph is usually stronger than three.

### Spokesperson escalation

- Default: Cynthia speaks for herself.
- If Cynthia is unavailable or the topic is personal: **Communications lead speaks on record.**
- If topic is legal: **No comment** + referral to an attorney. Never DIY legal commentary.
- If topic is about the campaign's funding or compliance: **Treasurer speaks.**

### Crisis-comms drill (run once before launch)

At a pre-launch team meeting, run a tabletop exercise:
- Moderator reads one of Attacks A-F.
- Team has 30 minutes to draft a response.
- Team critiques.
- Refine the pre-drafted counter.

Do this at least once. It cuts real-world response time from 4 hours to 60 minutes.

### After the response

- Log every attack + response in a shared tracker (date, source, medium, response posted, pickup, outcome).
- 48 hours later: assess what worked. Update the counter library.
- No response is thrown out. Every one becomes a template for the next.
