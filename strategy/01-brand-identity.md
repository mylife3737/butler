# Brand Identity

**Constraint:** $0 design budget. Canva Free + a decent photo of Cynthia is the entire toolkit until donations fund better.

**North star:** Mamdani-style authenticity. The brand isn't "polished political candidate." It's *this specific woman, this specific life, running against this specific broker*. Every design choice either supports that or is wrong.

---

## 1. Campaign name — three options

Present all three to Cynthia via `INTAKE-FORM.md §13`; she picks.

**Option A — "Cynthia Butler for Florida"**
- *Pro:* Warm, first-name-forward. Scales if she runs for a different office later. Matches the Mamdani "Zohran for NYC" pattern.
- *Con:* Doesn't signal the specific office. Voters may be unclear what she's running for.

**Option B — "Butler for District 75"**
- *Pro:* Signals office clearly. Ballot-name-forward if "Butler" is her surname on the ballot.
- *Con:* District numbers don't mean much to voters who don't know theirs. Cold.

**Option C — "Cynthia for Us"** (or "Our Cynthia")
- *Pro:* Solidaristic. Owns the "she's one of us" frame. Shorter URL.
- *Con:* Risk of sounding corny. Needs great typography to not feel like a small-business slogan.

**Recommendation:** Option A. Use "Cynthia Butler for Florida State Representative, District 75" as the legal/formal name on DS-DE 9 and all disclaimers; use "Cynthia Butler for Florida" as the public wordmark.

## 2. Taglines — three options

**Tagline 1 (biographical / earned credibility):**
> **"Seen it. Fixed it. Ready to change it."**
- Works because: paramedic-nurse credibility built into three verbs.

**Tagline 2 (contrast / populist):**
> **"He sells the land. I saved the lives."**
- Works because: hard contrast with Nix (commercial real-estate broker) in 7 words.
- Use carefully — needs pairing with positive content so it doesn't come off as only-attack.

**Tagline 3 (values / coalition):**
> **"Neighbors before donors."**
- Works because: anti-corruption-adjacent without being hectoring. Mamdani-adjacent tone.

**Recommendation:** Lead public content with **Tagline 1** ("Seen it. Fixed it. Ready to change it."). Use **Tagline 2** as the contrast line in direct-comparison content. Use **Tagline 3** as the sign-off on fundraising emails and the footer of the website.

## 3. Visual system

### Color palette

- **Primary:** Deep blue (#0A2342 or similar — not the DNC primary blue; a more coastal, slate-leaning version).
- **Accent:** Warm coral / sunrise orange (#E07856 or similar). Evokes dawn-shift EMS, Florida coast, and differentiates from every generic blue political sign.
- **Neutral:** Off-white / cream (#F4EDE4) for backgrounds.

Rationale: Most FL Republican signs are red; most FL Democratic signs are navy/white. A coral accent is immediately recognizable on a yard sign against a background of Nix signs. **Distinctiveness > convention.**

### Typography

- **Headline font:** A bold condensed sans-serif (Canva Free has "Bebas Neue" or "Oswald" — use one, don't mix).
- **Body font:** A readable humanist serif or modern sans — Lora (free) or Inter (free).
- **Use size and weight, not colors, to signal hierarchy.** Black / white / coral is enough.

### Photo direction

Three photo categories, all shot with whatever phone you have:

1. **Working photos** — Cynthia in a nursing uniform, paramedic jump bag, at a kitchen table with a bill in her hand, at a patient's bedside (de-identified / with permission). **Do not stage** — shoot actual moments.
2. **Community photos** — at an event, at a senior center, at a diner booth with supporters. Horizontal eye contact; no podium shots.
3. **Portrait** — one good head-and-shoulders photo for press kits. Natural light, warm expression, no over-retouching. Front-porch or fire-station backdrop, not a studio.

**Never use:** stock photos of the Florida Capitol, flag-waving hero shots, chopper-shot aerials of the district. They make every first-time candidate look like every other first-time candidate.

### Logo

Until a volunteer with Canva experience (or a small donation) lets us upgrade:

- Wordmark-only logo: **"Cynthia Butler for Florida"** in the bold condensed sans-serif, single color (deep blue), with a small coral underline or paramedic-cross accent. No icon.
- Deliverables: SVG, PNG (transparent + white background), black-only version for print.

## 5. Voice & tone guardrails

- **Do:** First-person, concrete, specific (name the neighborhood, the bill number, the storm).
- **Do:** Short sentences. Florida-English. Talk like a person who has been on 3 AM shifts.
- **Do:** Name Nix when attacking, not "my opponent" (earned-media SEO matters even in print).
- **Don't:** Use the word "progressive" unless Cynthia vetoed otherwise in `INTAKE-FORM.md §9.3`. It doesn't poll well in HD 75. Say "Florida Democrat" or just "Democrat."
- **Don't:** Use national-D-party buzzwords (equity, lived experience, marginalized, intersectional). They scan as imported.
- **Don't:** Over-explain the ambulance / nursing framework. Let it breathe.
- **Never:** Lie, embellish, or exaggerate the story. The whole thing collapses if one line turns out to be overstated.
