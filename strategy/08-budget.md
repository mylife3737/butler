## Budget tiers (if fundraising unlocks more)

When cumulative raised clears each threshold, deploy the next tier of tactics. Every deployment requires treasurer sign-off.

### Tier 1: +$500 raised (~Sprint 2)
- **First deployment: P2P texting.** Use [Strive](https://www.strivedigital.org/) or [ThruText](https://www.thrutext.com/) free tier / per-message pricing. ~$0.03–$0.08 per message. A 5,000-message campaign ≈ $150–$400. **TCPA guardrails apply** — only text to numbers with prior express consent (i.e., opted-in supporters). To non-opted-in numbers, use manual peer-to-peer tooling only, and follow the platform's vetting process.
- **Opt-in list-building starts Week 1:** every email signup form, every door intake, every event signup captures a cell phone with a clear "yes to text" checkbox.

### Tier 2: +$2,000 raised (~Sprint 3)
- More yard signs (another 200 pieces).
- Printed door-hangers for neighborhoods we can't fully canvass.
- Digital: **small Facebook post-boost ($25–$50)** to amplify 1-2 best-performing organic posts to local geotargeted audiences. DO NOT run generic "political ads" (platform vetting nightmare for small campaigns) — just promote content that's already performing.

### Tier 3: +$5,000 raised (~Sprint 4)
- Larger yard signs, including 4x4 feet for high-traffic corners (need landowner permission).
- A meaningful direct-mail piece: 1,500 households targeted, ~$900 all-in.
- Spanish-language professional translation of key materials (~$200-$400).
- Event photographer for launch + debate night (~$200).
- P2P texting at 10K-message scale (~$400-$800).

### Tier 4: +$15,000 raised (~Sprint 4-5)
- Targeted digital ads (geotargeted Facebook + YouTube pre-roll). ~$2,500.
- Second direct-mail piece (~$1,000).
- Paid radio on WGCU underwriting-style spots (~$300-$600).
- Upgrade CRM to a real voter-file-integrated tool like Action Network ($30/mo) or NGP VAN (often free via state party).

### Tier 5: +$40,000 raised (unlikely but planned)
- Cable or streaming TV buy (targeted, not broadcast — Hulu / YouTube TV / Roku political).
- Significant printed mail program (3-4 rounds of targeted mail).
- Paid canvassing staff (last 30 days only).

## P2P texting — first-tier-up tactic (deep dive)

Because this is the first tactic to deploy after Tier 1 is reached, it gets extra attention here.

**Why P2P first:**
- Cheapest persuasion-conversation tactic per contact.
- Opt-in list compliant → legal without risk.
- Works for event invites, VBM chase, GOTV, donation asks.
- No per-month minimum on most platforms (pay per message).

**Vendor comparison (confirm 2026 pricing before signup):**
- **Strive** — ~$0.03-$0.05 per message; integrates with most voter files; startup-friendly UI; actually endorses state-house candidate pricing.
- **ThruText (Spoke)** — ~$0.05-$0.10 per message; more enterprise-heavy; used by presidential campaigns.
- **RumbleUp** — similar pricing; Republican-aligned, skip.
- **Hustle** — similar pricing; legacy; still viable.
- ⚠️ VERIFY free-tier / startup pricing with each vendor at signup; do not rely on the rates above as gospel.

**Legal guardrails (not optional):**
- **Prior Express Consent** required for any autodialed text per TCPA.
- P2P tooling is technically manual-send, which is the exemption — but the platform's "send" button must be individually clicked by a human. Automated sending is NOT P2P.
- Every text includes an opt-out line: *"Reply STOP to unsubscribe."*
- Every text includes campaign disclaimer: *"Paid for and approved by Cynthia Butler for Florida, Democrat, for State Representative District 75."*

**Opt-in list-building starts now (pre-Tier 1):**
- Every volunteer signup form: checkbox "I agree to receive text messages from the Butler campaign. Msg/data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out."
- Every door canvas intake form: same consent line.
- Every event attendee signup: same consent line.
- At time of Tier 1 deployment, aim for 500+ opted-in phone numbers.

## Things explicitly NOT in the budget

- ❌ Political consultants (burn rate that kills grassroots campaigns).
- ❌ Polling firms (DIY cheap polling later if needed).
- ❌ Video production (phone + CapCut covers it).
- ❌ Paid door-knockers (volunteers only until Tier 5).
- ❌ Billboard / transit ads.
- ❌ Hotel / travel for Cynthia to Tallahassee. The campaign is the district.
- ❌ Swag (t-shirts, mugs, keychains). They don't persuade voters. If a supporter wants merch, sell it at cost through a tier-up revenue stream in Sprint 4+.
