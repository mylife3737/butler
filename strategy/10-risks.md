# Risks

Top 5 marketing risks with leading indicators + mitigations. Reviewed at every month-end roll-up.

---

## Risk 1 — Structural: R+28 district means even a well-executed campaign may not produce a win

**Leading indicators:**
- District-level polling (if we commission any) showing sub-40% for Cynthia by Labor Day.
- Amendment 4 / Amendment 3 HD-75 precinct splits (when pulled) showing the district was more red than statewide on those measures.
- No measurable state-D-wave materializing (statewide Gov / US Sen / Cabinet races).

**Mitigation:**
- Reframe "success" early — see `00-HYPOTHESES.md`: baseline success is closing the margin by 10+ points and building permanent coalition infrastructure.
- Communicate this realistically to supporters from Day 1. Over-promising sets up demoralization.
- Build list + brand as an asset that survives the 2026 result (grounds for 2028 run or local-office run).
- Target every tactic at the persuasion universe, not hard-R base (efficient use of $1K).

---

## Risk 2 — Candidate burnout

**Leading indicators:**
- Cynthia's hours-per-week tracking below INTAKE §3.3 target for 2 consecutive weeks.
- Missed events on the calendar.
- Visible decline in video/photo energy.
- Shortened or skipped Sunday rest day.
- Health signals (she was a paramedic — she knows the warning signs; take her self-report seriously).

**Mitigation:**
- Sunday is fully off — non-negotiable.
- Every 30 days: mandatory 48-hour full disconnect.
- Manager + Cynthia check in about time allocation monthly.
- Volunteer coordinator builds "surrogate Cynthia" capacity: 2-3 people who can do forums / events when she genuinely can't.
- Mental-health resources: Cynthia has healthcare access as a retired provider; manager reminds her it's available.

---

## Risk 3 — Compliance miss / fine

**Leading indicators:**
- Late TR report filing.
- Any expense paid without treasurer pre-approval.
- Missing disclaimer on published material.
- In-kind contribution (volunteer using personal funds for anything) not logged.
- Volunteer answering press inquiry.

**Mitigation:**
- `11-compliance.md` checklist reviewed Sprint 0; treasurer onboarding includes reading the FL Candidate Treasurer Handbook.
- Disclaimer text pre-drafted and pasted into every published piece (website footer, email footer, social post template, mailer, yard sign, P2P text).
- Treasurer pre-approves every check / ActBlue transfer / in-kind claim.
- One named human handles press; volunteers direct inquiries to that person.

---

## Risk 4 — Personal-attack vulnerability + opposition research surprise

**Leading indicators:**
- Attack content circulated on social, in LTEs, or in Nix campaign material.
- Reporter asks about a topic from Cynthia's personal history.
- Anonymous tips to local media.

**Mitigation:**
- `14-cynthia-self-audit.md` (PRIVATE) — pre-emptive inventory of any disclosable-before-weaponized issues.
- Proactive disclosure strategy: anything serious gets surfaced by the campaign first, in Cynthia's voice.
- Rapid-response SOP in `17-launch-and-crisis.md` with pre-drafted responses to the 5 likeliest attacks.
- Surrogate list (3 people from INTAKE §7.1) briefed to defend Cynthia publicly within hours of any attack.

---

## Risk 5 — Volunteer funnel stall

**Leading indicators:**
- 3 weeks under 10 new volunteer signups.
- Active volunteer count flat or declining.
- Known volunteers going 3+ weeks without a shift.
- Leadership meetings with <80% attendance for 2 consecutive weeks.

**Mitigation:**
- Volunteer coordinator calls any disengaged volunteer within 7 days of the silent period.
- Weekly "bring a friend" ask at every volunteer huddle.
- Launch + house parties + endorsement rollouts are recruitment moments — plan them around the funnel curve (`06-volunteer-funnel.md`).
- If funnel stalls despite these interventions: call the team together, diagnose (Is it messaging? Morale? Time of year? A specific negative incident?), and deliberately invest in rebuilding momentum (e.g., a focused recruitment drive at the next Charlotte DEC meeting).

## Secondary risks (monitored but not top-5)

- **Hurricane during campaign.** Sep/Oct 2026 storm hits HD 75 → pause campaigning, pivot to relief. Cynthia's paramedic background makes this a natural pivot that strengthens brand rather than weakening it. Pre-draft "pause" announcement.
- **Nix campaign outspends 10-to-1.** Expected; plan doesn't depend on spend parity.
- **A second Democrat files in primary.** Splits list and dollars. Unlikely but pre-draft a respectful cooperation outreach if it happens.
- **Cynthia's employer imposes restrictions** (see INTAKE §2.6). If yes, adjust public-facing content accordingly.
- **A key volunteer or treasurer quits.** Cross-train roles; no critical function has only one backup.
- **Social media platform ban / restriction** of political content. Known pattern in election years. Maintain multi-platform presence (don't depend on any single platform).
- **Cynthia's 365-day D registration invalid.** Fatal if true. Verify in Sprint 0 and again before qualifying.
