# Earned Media Playbook

**Thesis:** With $0 paid ad budget, earned media is the only scalable channel to reach voters who will never be door-knocked or personally networked. Every press placement is worth ~$1,500 in equivalent paid reach at local rates.

Target: 20+ confirmed placements from announcement through Election Day (print, TV, radio, digital, podcast).

**Every single placement requires:**
1. A clean one-page bio (Canva PDF, under 500 words).
2. A high-res campaign photo (300+ DPI, 3000px+ horizontal).
3. One quotable line ready to paste.
4. A named human point-of-contact (not "campaign@").

Without those three assets, no pitch goes out. They are Sprint 0 deliverables.

## Story angles (5 core arcs the campaign repeats all cycle)

### Angle 1 — "Paramedic to politician" (announcement + Month 1)
Biography-forward. "First-time candidate, spent X years as a paramedic/nurse, now running against a commercial realtor." High pickup rate because it's a clean human-interest hook.
- **Target outlets:** Charlotte Sun profile, Herald-Tribune feature, NBC2 package, WGCU Gulf Coast Life, Florida Politics blurb.
- **Quotable line draft:** *"I've been the person you call when you're having the worst day of your life. Now I want to be the person working to make sure more Floridians don't have one."*

### Angle 2 — "Insurance contrast" (Months 2-4)
Tie Nix's real-estate / EDP background to insurance industry ties. If campaign finance pull shows insurance-industry donors to Nix, this angle becomes a full investigative pitch.
- **Target outlets:** Florida Phoenix (investigative), Tampa Bay Times bureau, Suncoast Searchlight if active.
- **Quotable line draft:** *"My opponent's campaign is funded by the same developers and insurers making Charlotte County unaffordable. I don't answer to them. I answer to my neighbors."*

### Angle 3 — "Hurricane accountability" (anniversary-driven: Ian = Sep 28, Helene/Milton = Sep/Oct)
Timed around storm anniversaries and 2026 hurricane-season milestones.
- **Target outlets:** every TV station (NBC2, Fox 4, WINK, WWSB), every paper (Sun, Herald-Tribune), every radio.
- **Quotable line draft:** *"Three years ago, I was running calls while Ian made landfall. Three years later, my neighbors are still waiting. That's not a recovery. That's abandonment."*

### Angle 4 — "The overdevelopment question" (Months 3-5)
Wellen Park + West Villages + 2050 Plan fights + Nix's EDP presidency. Policy-heavy, needs a specific bill or vote to be pickup-worthy.
- **Target outlets:** Herald-Tribune (historically covered growth), Florida Politics, Charlotte Sun.
- **Quotable line draft:** *"Every new subdivision is another family who can't afford a starter home. We've built ourselves out of our own community."*

### Angle 5 — "Medicaid expansion / rural EMS" (Months 3-5 + GOTV stretch)
Closing-argument healthcare theme. Cynthia's single most distinctive credential.
- **Target outlets:** WGCU, WUSF, Florida Phoenix, Daily Kos-type progressive outlets for list-building.
- **Quotable line draft:** *"In the district I'll represent, help can be 22 minutes away. The people in Tallahassee who could fix that are worried about book bans instead. I'm not."*

## Letter-to-editor (LTE) program

LTEs are the single highest-ROI earned-media tactic for this budget. Rules of the road:

- **Rotating stable of 10-15 supporters** writes LTEs. Cynthia writes 1-2/month; everyone else drives volume.
- **No form letters.** Each writer draws on their own experience. Templates below are frames, not scripts.
- **One letter per author per 30 days per outlet** (most outlets enforce this).
- **Submit to multiple outlets in parallel** — a letter rejected by the Herald-Tribune can be rewritten for the Sun.
- **Local hook required** — reference a specific street, neighborhood, school, hospital, storm.

### LTE template pack (starter frames)

**Frame A — "insurance"**
> My insurance premium went from $X to $Y in two years. I called Rep. Nix's office. I called his commercial real-estate firm. I got silence. Cynthia Butler came to my door. She's running for HD 75 because we deserve a representative who answers the phone. — [Name, city]

**Frame B — "Ian recovery"**
> It's been [X] years since Ian. There are still tarps on my street in [neighborhood]. State government moved on; we haven't. Cynthia Butler was on the back of an ambulance the night Ian hit. She knows what she saw. She'll be a representative who remembers. — [Name, city]

**Frame C — "healthcare / EMS"**
> When my mother had her stroke, it took 19 minutes for help to arrive. That's not safe. Tallahassee has refused to expand Medicaid, refused to fund rural EMS, refused to listen. Cynthia Butler has done this work for 20 years. She'll make Tallahassee listen. — [Name, city]

**Frame D — "schools"**
> I moved to Port Charlotte for the schools. Now we lose teachers every year because Tallahassee treats us like a punching bag. Cynthia Butler put herself through school on Pell grants. She'll fight for ours. — [Name, city]

**Frame E — "overdevelopment"**
> I grew up in Charlotte County. I can't afford to retire here anymore. The incumbent is a commercial realtor who ran the Economic Development Partnership. That's the problem. Cynthia Butler is the solution. — [Name, city]

## Rapid-response framework

When Nix's campaign or allies attack — and they will — the cycle is:

1. **Hour 0-2:** comms lead + Cynthia decide: respond publicly, respond quietly, or ignore.
2. **Hour 2-4:** if responding publicly, draft a 2-sentence response using the message box (`15-message-box.md`). Do NOT deny; reframe.
3. **Hour 4-6:** ship the response on the channel where the attack landed (Facebook post, press statement, LTE).
4. **Hour 6-24:** monitor pickup. If it grows, consider 1 additional statement or a longer-form post. If it dies, stop feeding it.

**Default:** most attacks are better ignored than amplified. The question to ask before every response: *"Does a neutral voter learn more about us or more about them if we respond?"*

See `17-launch-and-crisis.md` for the pre-drafted attack/counter library.

## Outlet-specific tactics

- **Florida Politics "Sunburn":** they take short blurbs; include a photo and a URL. Pitch before 7 AM for next-day inclusion.
- **Charlotte Sun + Sarasota Herald-Tribune:** longer-form; offer a sit-down interview 3-7 days before the story needs to run.
- **NBC2 / Fox 4 / WWSB:** need B-roll. Offer Cynthia at a visually interesting setting (ambulance bay, clinic, neighborhood with storm damage).
- **WGCU Gulf Coast Life:** segment runs ~10 min; needs a clear policy hook and a compelling personal story.
- **WSLR Sarasota:** candidate-friendly; use for early on-mic practice.
- **Podcasts:** pitch after a news peg or after an endorsement.

---

## What to NEVER do

- ❌ Lie to a reporter. Ever. One lie = permanent reputation damage = campaign over.
- ❌ Go off the record without explicitly saying "this is off the record" AND the reporter confirming.
- ❌ Share a document with a reporter assuming it won't be published.
- ❌ Trash-talk other Democrats on the record.
- ❌ Pitch a story you don't have documentation for.
- ❌ Send the same pitch to 10 outlets in a single CC'd email (always BCC or personalize).

---

## Measurement

- Weekly: count placements (print/TV/radio/digital). Log each with URL, date, outlet, reach estimate, sentiment.
- Monthly: calculate estimated "paid equivalent" (placements × outlet's rate card).
- Trigger: if Sprint 2 yields fewer than 5 placements, re-examine pitch quality + outlet targeting with the team.
