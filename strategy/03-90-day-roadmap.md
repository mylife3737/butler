**Planning horizon:** 2026-04-17 (today) → 2026-11-03 (Election Day) — approximately 200 days, or roughly six 30-day sprints.
**Critical dates (sourced in `../research/01-district-fact-sheet.md`):**
- **Qualifying window:** noon Mon June 8, 2026 → noon Fri June 12, 2026.
- **Primary:** Tue Aug 18, 2026.
- **General:** Tue Nov 3, 2026.
- **365-day party rule deadline already passed.** Cynthia's D registration must predate June 8, 2025. *Verify this week.*

This is a 6-sprint plan. Each sprint opens with a Monday kickoff (goals, owners) and closes with a Friday retro (KPIs vs. plan; decisions for next sprint).

**Objective:** soft launch + qualifying.

- [ ] **File qualifying paperwork during the June 8-12 window.** Bring certified copy of DS-DE 9, filing fee OR petition signatures if using that path (verify with Charlotte County SOE — state house qualifying fee is ~$1,781 in 2024; confirm 2026 fee).
- [ ] First 10 "coffee with Cynthia" 1:1 meetings with future donors + endorsers.
- [ ] Submit first endorsement application — **Ruth's List Florida** (supports women D candidates running for state office; apply before they make their summer slate decisions).
- [ ] Submit application: **Emerge Florida** (if Cynthia is program-eligible — free training + network).
- [ ] Launch website (just a splash page + email capture until Sprint 2).
- [ ] Build initial email list — target 100 subscribers by end of Sprint 1.
- [ ] Private soft launch: Cynthia sends a personal text / call to her top 50 network contacts (from `INTAKE-FORM.md §4.1-4.2`) with the ask: "I'm running. Here's why. Will you donate $X / volunteer / host a house party?" Track responses in the CRM spreadsheet.
- [ ] Do not go public on social yet; that's Sprint 2's launch.

**Exit criteria:** qualified for ballot. 50 email subscribers. 25 first-line supporters committed to an ask (donation / volunteer / host).

## Sprint 3 — Build (July 16 → August 18, 2026 / Primary Election Day)

**Objective:** primary day (likely uncontested D primary — verify in `../research/01`), intensify door program, house-party cadence, first VBM chase wave.

- [ ] Increase canvass to 3-4 shifts/week. Target 300 doors/week.
- [ ] Weekly house party. By end of Sprint 3: 4 parties completed, ~60 new supporters per party on average.
- [ ] Launch P2P texting (free tier) to opted-in supporters — event invites + volunteer asks.
- [ ] First email-list segmentation: "engaged" (opened 2+ emails) vs. "cold" (opened 0). Treat differently.
- [ ] First VBM push: deliver a VBM request card to every Democrat + persuadable NPA household knocked.
- [ ] Faith-community outreach begins — see `18-underserved-outreach.md`. Target 1 Sunday appearance by end of Sprint 3.
- [ ] Spanish-language content pilot — 1 translated post per week.
- [ ] Primary night (Aug 18): rally supporters for "pivot to November" event, even if uncontested. Creates a media moment and lets supporters see progress.

**Exit criteria:** 1,000 email subscribers. 2,000 doors knocked total. 6 LTEs published. $6,000+ raised. 30 active volunteers.

## Sprint 5 — GOTV (October 1 → November 3, 2026)

**Objective:** turnout, turnout, turnout. Every tactic oriented at "get this voter to return their ballot or show up."

- [ ] VBM chase universe launched: door + call + text every Democrat and persuadable NPA who requested a VBM ballot until they return it.
- [ ] Early voting begins (dates vary by county — verify). Drive rides-to-polls for committed-D low-propensity voters.
- [ ] Final round of handwritten postcards to top 500 target households (~$250 postage).
- [ ] "Closing argument" week-before video + email (the one piece Cynthia records that tells her full story in 90 seconds).
- [ ] Election Day operations: poll captains, sample ballots at poll lines (outside 150-ft exclusion zone per FL law), ride-to-polls line manned 7 AM – 7 PM.
- [ ] Win or lose, prep supporter thank-you night of Nov 3 + next-morning email.

**Exit criteria:** actual execution. KPIs become turnout numbers (see `09-kpis.md` for full list).
