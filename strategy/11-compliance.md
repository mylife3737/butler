# Compliance — Florida Ch. 106 Campaign Finance

**READ THIS BEFORE RAISING OR SPENDING A SINGLE DOLLAR.**

First-time candidates in Florida get hit with fines every cycle for small, avoidable errors — missing disclaimers, late reports, unlogged in-kinds, personal-funds commingling. Every one is preventable if the treasurer follows a checklist.

**Primary source:** [FL Candidate & Campaign Treasurer Handbook](https://files.floridados.gov/media/706923/candidate-and-campaign-treasurer-handbook-2024.pdf) (2024 edition; check for 2026 update).

**Secondary sources (also mandatory reading for the treasurer):**
- [FL DOE Qualifying Information](https://dos.fl.gov/elections/candidates-committees/qualifying/)
- [FL DOE Campaign Finance Database](https://dos.fl.gov/elections/candidates-committees/campaign-finance/campaign-finance-database/)

## Sprint 0 checklist (before raising or spending)

- [ ] Confirm Cynthia's 365-day D registration status (`../research/01-district-fact-sheet.md §F`).
- [ ] Confirm Cynthia is / will be a resident of FL for ≥2 years before election (statutory requirement for state representative — F.S. 99.012).
- [ ] Confirm Cynthia's residence address is within HD 75 boundaries OR will be by the time she takes office.
- [ ] **Appoint a campaign treasurer.** Cannot be Cynthia herself for practical purposes (legal but discouraged; creates conflicts + doubles workload). Treasurer should be:
  - FL resident and registered voter;
  - Not a convicted felon without rights restored;
  - Comfortable with accounting;
  - Willing to be liable for late/missing reports.
- [ ] **Designate a campaign depository.** Any FL-authorized bank, savings & loan, or credit union. This is a designation, not yet an account open.
- [ ] **Open the campaign depository account.** Separate from any personal account. Checking only; NOT interest-bearing. Some campaigns use Wells Fargo, some use local community banks. Nothing prohibits choice; prefer a bank Cynthia already banks with to simplify signatures.
- [ ] **File DS-DE 9** (Appointment of Campaign Treasurer and Designation of Campaign Depository) with the FL Division of Elections. For state legislative candidates, filed with DOE; some local races file with county SOE.
- [ ] **Within 10 days of DS-DE 9:** file the candidate statement of understanding (indicates Cynthia has read the candidate handbook).
- [ ] **Obtain campaign EIN** from IRS (free online at irs.gov/EIN — takes ~15 min).
- [ ] Register a campaign name that matches across DS-DE 9, depository account, EIN, website, ActBlue, and all communications. "Cynthia Butler for Florida State Representative District 75" is the full legal name recommended.

## Qualifying (June 8-12, 2026)

To get on the ballot, a state legislative candidate must either:

- **Pay the qualifying fee** to the Division of Elections (~$1,781 for state house in 2024; verify 2026), OR
- **Submit petition signatures** collected from HD 75 registered voters (quantity threshold varies; check current rule). This is a significant undertaking — a volunteer signature-drive team needs 30-60 days of lead time.

**Recommendation:** plan for the qualifying fee path; it's cleaner, and fundraising should cover the fee by June.

Within the June 8-12 window, Cynthia physically or electronically files:
- Notarized loyalty oath (F.S. 99.021)
- Qualifying form specific to state house (Form DS-DE 12 for state legislators)
- Financial disclosure form (Form 6 — full financial disclosure for state legislators per 2023 reform)
- Qualifying fee payment OR verified petitions

**⚠️ CRITICAL.** Form 6 requires public disclosure of Cynthia's net worth, liabilities, income sources — no redactions. Prepare emotionally and with an accountant. If Cynthia has items she'd rather not disclose publicly (see `14-cynthia-self-audit.md`), Form 6 is where they become public.

## Disclaimers — required on EVERYTHING

Every public-facing campaign material must include a political disclaimer per F.S. 106.143. The standard Democrat, state-house form:

> **Political advertisement paid for and approved by Cynthia Butler, Democrat, for State Representative, District 75.**

Variations accepted (e.g., "sponsored by," "authorized by"), but the core elements must appear:
- Who paid for it (the committee legal name).
- Which party (if partisan primary).
- Which office / district.
- Approval by the candidate.

**Placement rules:**
- **Mailers, printed literature, yard signs, postcards:** visible, readable.
- **Website footer:** on every page.
- **Email:** in every campaign email footer.
- **Social posts:** in the profile bio + in the post caption of any paid-boosted post. Organic posts on the campaign's own page are less strictly enforced but include it for defense.
- **P2P texts:** in the text message itself OR (if character-limited) in a linked landing page.
- **Radio / TV spots (if ever produced):** audio at the end, clearly spoken.
- **Verbal introductions at events:** Cynthia personally says "I'm Cynthia Butler, I approve this message" or analog at any broadcast-bound content.

**What happens if missing:** per-incident fines, "warning" letters, possible complaints from Nix's campaign that become news stories. Don't skip it.

## Fundraising rules

### Contribution limits (F.S. 106.08)
Per election (primary and general count separately), per person:
- To a state house candidate: **$3,000 per election** in 2024. ⚠️ Verify 2026 rule.
- So across primary + general = $6,000 total per donor.

### Prohibited sources
- **Corporations** (unless sole proprietorships) — F.S. 106.08.
- **Foreign nationals** — federal law (52 U.S.C. §30121).
- **Anonymous contributions over $50** — F.S. 106.09.
- **Cash contributions over $50** — F.S. 106.09.

### Contribution information required for all donors over $10
Name, address, occupation, employer (to be itemized on TR reports).

### Timing
- Every contribution must be deposited in the campaign depository within **5 business days** of receipt — F.S. 106.05.
- Every contribution must be logged in the treasurer's records.

## Spending rules

- Every expense paid from the depository only.
- No personal-funds commingling. If Cynthia pays for something out of pocket, it must be logged as a **loan** (repaid only to the extent the campaign raises funds) OR an **in-kind contribution** (counts against her personal contribution limit, which is unlimited to her own committee).
- Volunteer reimbursements only with receipt + pre-approved by treasurer.
- No expenditure made to a non-qualifying vendor without documentation.

---

## In-kind contributions (commonly missed)

Every time someone:
- Prints flyers on their printer at their expense.
- Hosts a house party and buys the food.
- Drives Cynthia around using their gas.
- Uses their personal cell phone minutes for campaign calls (rarely logged — but technically).

...that's an **in-kind contribution**, valued at fair-market-equivalent, and counts against that donor's contribution limit. Log every instance. Treasurer maintains in-kind ledger.

**Exception:** uncompensated volunteer time is NOT an in-kind. Only tangible goods/services with a dollar value.

## Reporting calendar (TR reports)

⚠️ 2026 EXACT DATES TO VERIFY. The pattern:
- Monthly reports required once you've filed DS-DE 9 until qualifying ends.
- After qualifying, reports become more frequent approaching elections (semi-monthly, then weekly last 30 days).
- Treasurer filings through the state's electronic filing system.

Late fees: **$50 per day** for each day late, up to the amount of receipts or expenditures not reported. Nobody absorbs this; prevention is the entire strategy.

**Treasurer owns a shared calendar with every TR due date. Manager has reminders 7 days, 3 days, and 1 day before each.**

## Debate + public forum compliance

- Any co-sponsored forum with Nix or other candidates must follow equal-access rules.
- Forums hosted on public property have First Amendment implications for who can speak.
- LWV candidate forums have specific rules (no personal attacks, moderated format).

---

## Election Day compliance

- **Campaigning within 150 feet of a polling location is prohibited** under F.S. 102.031(4). Sample ballots, signs, and candidates themselves must stay outside the zone.
- Poll watchers: each campaign can designate poll watchers per precinct; registration with county SOE required in advance.

---

## Post-election

- Final campaign finance report due [days] after election. Verify deadline.
- Campaign account can remain open to pay bills or return contributions.
- Must dispose of remaining funds per F.S. 106.141 (e.g., return to donors, donate to party, donate to specific charity, pay expenses).
