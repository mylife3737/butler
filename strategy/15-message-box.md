# Message Box

The single discipline that keeps a campaign's communications coherent. Every post, LTE, speech, door script, email, and surrogate talking point maps to one of four quadrants. If a piece of content doesn't fit a quadrant, it doesn't ship.

---

## The 4-quadrant box

```
                    ABOUT CYNTHIA                    ABOUT NIX
                    ──────────────                   ─────────
  WE SAY     | Q1: What we say about us      | Q2: What we say about him   |
  (positive  | (biographical + issue-record) | (contrast + record attacks) |
  + attack)  |                               |                             |
             |-------------------------------|-----------------------------|
  THEY SAY   | Q3: What they'll say about us | Q4: What they'll say about  |
  (defense   | (attacks to counter)          |     him (defenses to reframe)|
  + reframe) |                               |                             |
```

## Q1 — What WE say about CYNTHIA (positive self-narrative)

### Core frame
**"She's one of us, she's seen it up close, she fights for her neighbors."**

### Three biographical proof points (lock after INTAKE §1)
1. **Lived the crisis:** single mom, government-assistance-to-nursing-school journey. Not an abstract supporter of working families — a graduate of that path.
2. **Worked the crisis:** 20+ years as a paramedic/nurse in Charlotte County. Ran calls during Ian. Saw rural EMS response times. Delivered care to patients who couldn't afford it.
3. **Understands the crisis:** homeowner in HD 75. Insurance tripled. Knows teachers struggling, neighbors rebuilding after storms, seniors who can't afford medications.

### Three issue proof points
1. **Property insurance:** "I've been inside the houses the insurance companies denied. I'll make them pay what they owe."
2. **Hurricane recovery:** "I worked Ian. Three storms later, my neighbors still have tarps. That's a failure of representation."
3. **Rural EMS + Medicaid expansion:** "Your zip code decides how fast help arrives. It shouldn't. I'll make Tallahassee care about the parts of Florida it's forgotten."

### Core vocabulary (use)
- "Neighbor" (never "voter" when speaking to a voter).
- "Work" (paramedics and nurses do work — and so do teachers, waitresses, mechanics).
- "Fight" (contrasts with Nix's "advocate" / "sponsor" vocabulary).
- "Fix" (tied to Cynthia's medical / practical-solver identity).
- "Answer" (as in "I'll answer the phone when you call my office").

### Forbidden vocabulary (never use)
- "Progressive" (unless Cynthia explicitly reverses in INTAKE §9.3).
- "Equity," "lived experience," "intersectional," "marginalized" (national-brand jargon).
- "MAGA" (personalizes in a way that backfires with soft-R crossovers).
- "Radical right" (ditto).
- "Working-class" (ironically — tested poorly in similar SW Florida persuasion work; say "working" alone).

## Q2 — What WE say about NIX (contrast attack)

### Core frame
**"He sells the land. He runs the developer group. He votes with the donor class. He's never been in your kitchen."**

### Three factual attack lines (sourced per `../research/01-district-fact-sheet.md`)
1. **The commercial realtor attack:** "A commercial realtor voting on housing policy is a built-in conflict of interest. Every vote is a potential business interest."
2. **The Economic Development Partnership attack:** "He was the president of the developer group for Charlotte County. Ask yourself — does the state representative for Charlotte County work for the developers or for you?"
3. **The insurance / developer donor attack:** "His donors are the people making Florida unaffordable. Look at the filings."

### Three record attack lines (populate from 2025 voting record — currently `⚠️ UNVERIFIED`; fill after pulling FastDemocracy/LobbyTools)
1. [Vote on insurance] — "When Floridians needed relief, Nix voted with the insurance lobby." [Bill number + vote]
2. [Vote on housing / overdevelopment] — "Nix voted to make it easier to build, harder to afford." [Bill number]
3. [Vote on healthcare / Medicaid] — "Nix voted against your hospital." [Bill number]

### Rules of contrast
- **Name him.** "Danny Nix" or "Rep. Nix." Not "my opponent." SEO matters; earned-media mentions matter.
- **Always source.** Every vote claim has a bill number + a date + a roll-call link.
- **Don't personalize.** Attack the RECORD, the DONORS, the CONFLICT. Don't attack him personally (his family, his appearance, his religion).
- **Don't shout.** Matter-of-fact is more persuasive than indignant.

## Q3 — What THEY'LL say about CYNTHIA (attacks to counter)

Anticipated attacks + pre-drafted counters:

### Attack A: "She's inexperienced / never held office."
- **Counter:** "I've been in the rooms where people's lives change. I've run calls at 3 AM. I've put myself through school. Tallahassee has plenty of 'experienced' politicians. It's short on people who've actually worked."
- **Who delivers:** Cynthia herself, first person.

### Attack B: "She was on welfare / government assistance."
- **Counter (proactive disclosure wins this one — see `14-cynthia-self-audit.md`):** "Yes, I was. WIC and Pell. They worked — I paid my taxes back many times over. Now my opponent votes to cut them. That's the difference between someone who's been there and someone who hasn't."
- **Who delivers:** Cynthia herself, front-foot.

### Attack C: "She's a radical Democrat / socialist."
- **Counter:** "I'm a paramedic. I'm a nurse. I'm a Florida Democrat who believes your insurance shouldn't triple in two years. Call me whatever you want — I've got a job to do."
- **Who delivers:** Cynthia herself, short, dismissive.

### Attack D: "She'll raise your taxes."
- **Counter:** "My opponent voted to keep your insurance premiums going up. Ask who's raising what. I'll fight to lower the things you actually pay every month."

### Attack F: "She'll bring Biden / Kamala / Mamdani / NYC-style politics to Florida."

## Q4 — What THEY'LL say about NIX (defenses to reframe)

Nix's campaign will say positive things about him. We don't rebut facts; we reframe angle.

### Nix claim: "Lifelong Charlotte County business leader."
- **Reframe:** "Commercial realtor and developer-group president isn't 'business leader' — it's a particular kind of business interest. Ask what that business interest votes for."

### Nix claim: "Delivered for Charlotte County in his first term."
- **Reframe:** "Delivered what? Name a bill. Name a vote. His first-term record is thin. Mine would be full."

### Nix claim: "Will keep Florida red / conservative values."
- **Reframe:** "Your insurance premium doesn't care about red or blue. Your blue tarp doesn't care. Your kid's school doesn't care. I'll vote for what works."

### Nix claim: "Lowering taxes / cutting regulations."
- **Reframe:** "He and his party have been in full control of Tallahassee for 26 years. If 'lowering taxes' was going to fix your insurance, it would have by now."

## How to use the box

**Weekly:** comms lead audits last week's content — every post, email, LTE, press quote — and tags each with Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4. If 80%+ is Q1/Q2 (our framing), we're on track. If >50% is Q3/Q4 (reacting to them), we're letting them set the narrative — reset.

**Monthly:** re-read the box with Cynthia. Update counters based on what's actually been said. Retire attacks that didn't materialize; add new ones that did.

**Before any public appearance:** Cynthia rehearses 3 Q1 bullets, 2 Q2 bullets, and 1 pre-drafted counter to each likely attack from Q3.
