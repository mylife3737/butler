# Volunteer Funnel

The campaign's unpaid labor is its largest asset. The funnel is how strangers become voters who become supporters who become volunteers who become leaders. Without this working, the plan collapses — there's no paid-ads budget to substitute.

## The funnel

```
    SOURCE           →    SIGN-UP     →     ONBOARD     →    ACTIVATE    →    RETAIN
  (how they hear)      (give email)     (welcome)         (first task)      (next task)
```

### Source (how people hear about us)

Rank by conversion rate observed in comparable grassroots races:

1. **Personal ask by Cynthia or an existing volunteer** (~40% convert to signup). Always outperforms broadcast.
2. **Door-knocking conversations** (~12% of doors yield an email or volunteer signup).
3. **Launch event + subsequent events** (~30% of attendees sign up for something).
4. **Facebook page + organic social** (~1-3% of reach signs up).
5. **Endorsement announcements** (endorsing org cross-promotes to their list).
6. **Press / LTE / media hits** (small but real bump in signups every time a piece runs).

### Sign-up

- **One form, everywhere:** a single volunteer signup form (Google Form → Mailchimp → spreadsheet) linked from:
  - Website "Volunteer" button
  - Every email footer
  - Facebook page "Learn More" CTA
  - QR code on business cards + yard signs
  - Paper signup sheets at every event
- Fields: name, email, phone, zip, "what interests you" (checkboxes: doors, phones, data, writing, hosting, social media, events, legal/treasury, other), availability (days/times), skills/languages.

### Onboard (within 48 hours of signup)

- Automated welcome email (Mailchimp workflow): "Welcome. Here's what we're doing this week. Here's a specific thing you can do in the next 7 days." Include:
  - Link to the full strategy (the `00-README.md` summary, not the internals).
  - Next event date.
  - Two low-effort first asks: share this post + forward this email to 3 friends.
- Within 7 days: a real human (volunteer coordinator or Cynthia for high-value signups) calls or texts them.

### Activate (first real shift)

The "first shift" is the hinge. If a new volunteer doesn't do something tangible within 21 days of signup, their activation probability drops below 10%. Within 7 days it's still above 50%.

Starter-shift menu (pick the one matching their "what interests you" answer):

- **Doors:** Saturday canvass shift (3 hours, paired with experienced volunteer).
- **Phones:** Tuesday evening phone bank (2 hours, from home or in-person).
- **Data:** help clean the CRM spreadsheet (remote, 1 hour).
- **Writing:** submit an LTE using the template pack.
- **Hosting:** schedule a house-party date 30-45 days out.
- **Social:** reshare 3 posts this week + 1 photo from a local meetup.
- **Events:** show up at a community booth and hand out palm cards for 2 hours.
- **Legal / treasury:** the one role that should go to a known contact with specific qualifications.
- **Other:** 1-on-1 with coordinator to figure out best fit.

### Retain (4-shift ladder)

| Shifts | Status | What they get |
|---|---|---|
| 1 shift | New volunteer | Thank-you text from Cynthia personally (within 24 hours). |
| 2-3 shifts | Regular | Add to weekly volunteer huddle; invite to "inner circle" group chat. |
| 4-5 shifts | Committed | Offer a named role ("neighborhood captain," "LTE coordinator," "phone bank host"). |
| 6+ shifts | Leader | Invite to weekly leadership meeting with Cynthia; trust with real decision authority. |

## Weekly volunteer cadence

- **Monday:** coordinator sends the week's "shifts available" email.
- **Wednesday + Saturday:** canvass shifts (ramping 2 → 4 shifts/week through the cycle).
- **Tuesday + Thursday:** phone bank shifts (starting Month 3).
- **Friday:** coordinator 1:1 check-ins with 5 randomly-chosen active volunteers. Keeps a pulse on morale.
- **Sunday evening:** 30-minute "week ahead" volunteer call. Open to anyone.

## Leadership roles to fill

Target by end of Sprint 2:

1. **Campaign manager** — owns everything, ideally Cynthia's closest trusted volunteer.
2. **Treasurer** — compliance, reporting, bank reconciliation. Must be separate from Cynthia.
3. **Field captain** — doors + events + turf-cutting.
4. **Communications lead** — email + press + LTEs.
5. **Social media lead** — 5+ posts/week, comment moderation.
6. **Volunteer coordinator** — the funnel described here.
7. **Finance / house-party chair** — donor calls + event planning.
8. **Data / digital lead** — voter file, CRM, website.

People can double up to start. By Sprint 4, ideally 8 distinct humans.

## Recruitment hacks

- **"Who's that smart person from your life"** exercise — at every volunteer meeting, each person writes down 3 names of people who'd be good at one of the open roles. That list becomes next-week's personal-ask target.
- **House parties double as recruitment.** Every party yields 1-3 new committed volunteers on average.
- **Local Democratic clubs** — attend monthly meetings of the Charlotte DEC, Sarasota DEC, and all the local Democratic Women's Clubs. Cynthia introduces herself + asks for volunteer signups at every one.
- **Unions** — IAFF local (fire/EMS), AFSCME, teachers union. Even if no endorsement materializes, individual members often volunteer.
- **Former campaigns** — past Dunbar (2024 D nominee) volunteers are a warm list. If Dunbar will share contacts, high-priority.

## Volunteer retention red flags

- Volunteer goes 3 weeks without a shift → coordinator calls them personally.
- New signups not onboarded within 48 hours → coordinator failure; escalate.
- Leadership meeting has <80% attendance for 2 weeks → morale issue; Cynthia convenes emotional check-in.

---

## Explicit do-nots

- ❌ Do not let volunteers knock doors solo their first 2 shifts. Pair them.
- ❌ Do not let volunteers answer press inquiries. Route to comms lead.
- ❌ Do not let volunteers handle donations directly (money flows through ActBlue or the treasurer only).
- ❌ Do not ask volunteers to pay for anything out-of-pocket without pre-approval from treasurer (in-kind contribution compliance — see `11-compliance.md`).
