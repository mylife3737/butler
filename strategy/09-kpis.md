# KPIs

**Cadence:** weekly review every Friday, 60 minutes, manager + Cynthia. Monthly roll-up the first Friday of each month.

**Philosophy:** Track inputs (actions we control) more than outputs (voters' decisions). We can't move the vote directly; we can move the contacts, signups, dollars, and placements.

## Monthly roll-up

Additional metrics reviewed monthly (first Friday):

- Cumulative doors knocked
- Cumulative unique voter conversations (per voter-file ID)
- Cumulative donors
- Cumulative raised
- Cumulative placements (paid-equivalent value)
- Cumulative LTEs published
- Volunteer funnel: new / active / leader counts
- Cash on hand vs. burn rate (treasurer reports)
- Compliance status (TR reports filed on time, no outstanding fines)
- Any hypothesis updates (see `00-HYPOTHESES.md` for protocol)

## Election Day metrics

On Nov 3:
- Polls opened without incident (yes/no).
- Poll captains present at all key precincts (yes/no).
- Ride-to-polls rides delivered.
- GOTV call/text volume (dials, texts, doors).
- Results: Cynthia's share, Nix's share, margin, turnout.

---

## Red-flag triggers

Any of these require immediate (same-week) manager+Cynthia meeting to decide action:

- 3 consecutive weeks of email growth under 15 new signups.
- 3 consecutive weeks of door totals under 100.
- Any week with $0 raised after Sprint 2.
- Any volunteer-onboarding breakdown (new signups going >7 days without contact).
- Any compliance miss (late TR report, missing disclaimer on material).
- Any negative press story without a rapid response executed in-cycle.
- Cynthia's personal hours-per-week tracking below the INTAKE §3.3 target for 2 consecutive weeks.

---

## What we are NOT measuring

- Vanity metrics: Facebook "reach" without engagement, email open rate in isolation, Twitter followers.
- Things we can't influence: opponent's fundraising (useful to know but not a KPI for us).
- National polling or generic-D-ballot indicators (they don't apply to a R+28 district).
