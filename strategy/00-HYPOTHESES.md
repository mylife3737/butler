# Strategic Hypotheses

Three beliefs drive the entire plan. They are falsifiable. Every 30 days we re-examine them against the data we've collected and update the strategy accordingly.

---

## H1 — The contrast hypothesis

**Claim:** In HD 75, a paramedic/nurse/single-mom Democrat running against a commercial real-estate broker Republican, on a message of "property insurance + hurricane recovery + rural EMS + overdevelopment," can close Nix's 27.9-point margin to under 15 points — and in a strong D-wave year could win.

**Why we believe it:**
- Amendment 4 (abortion) drew 57% statewide in Nov 2024 while Harris lost by double digits. Florida voters split-ticket.
- Amendment 3 (cannabis) drew 56%. Same pattern.
- Together: this is a libertarian-populist electorate, not a lockstep-R electorate. The gap between "Trump voters" and "status-quo-R-incumbent voters" is persuadable.
- Nix is a first-term legislator with a developer / economic-development presidency on his record. The conflict-of-interest frame writes itself.
- Cynthia's biography is a Mamdani-grade contrast: working-class credential + lived healthcare experience + single-mom story. No one else in HD 75 has that profile on the D side.

**What would falsify it:**
- A district-level poll (even a cheap P2P one of 200 voters) showing the abortion/cannabis split doesn't map to HD 75.
- Cynthia's on-camera / door performance testing poorly (→ fall back to written-media strategy).
- Nix avoiding developer-friendly votes during the 2026 session (less contrast ammo).

**How we test:**
- Month 1: pull Amendment 4 / Amendment 3 county-level splits for Charlotte and Sarasota (Englewood precincts).
- Month 1-2: door-knock 500 doors with a 3-question survey (top issue, opinion of incumbent, open to a D challenger). Cost: $0.
- Month 2-3: run a $25 Facebook post-boost on 3 message variants; measure engagement rate as a cheap A/B on framing.

## H2 — The list-to-dollars hypothesis

**Claim:** With zero paid advertising, we can build a 2,500-person email list and a 400-donor small-dollar base by Nov 3, 2026, entirely through organic content + door contact + house parties + earned media.

**Why we believe it:**
- Mamdani built NYC-scale lists on organic + short-form video + volunteer army. HD 75 is ~1/30th the scale — the math is more forgiving, not less.
- ActBlue's average Florida state-house small-dollar donor gives ~$40 and gives 2.3 times per cycle (⚠️ benchmark — refine with actual ActBlue data). 400 donors × $40 × 2.3 = ~$37K raised. Well above the $1K ceiling on our own marketing spend, but it's the list itself that's the asset.
- Every door-knock leaves an email address if the script works. 20 volunteers × 10 doors/week × 30 weeks × 40% email-capture = 2,400 emails.

**What would falsify it:**
- Volunteer recruitment stalling below 20 active at any point.
- Door email-capture rate under 15% after Week 6.
- Email list growing but conversion-to-donor rate below 5% by Labor Day.

**How we test:**
- Weekly KPIs in `09-kpis.md` track list size, donor count, and volunteer active-count every Friday. Three consecutive red weeks on any of these triggers a strategy review.

## H3 — The coalition-first hypothesis

**Claim:** Endorsements + 3 underserved constituency tracks (Spanish-language voters, Black faith communities, the recovery community) deliver more net vote-shift per hour of candidate time than any paid-media dollar we could spend.

**Why we believe it:**
- Endorsements come with donor lists and volunteer pipelines (Ruth's List, Emerge FL, FEA, unions). They're force multipliers, not vanity.
- The Latino share of North Port and Port Charlotte has grown materially since 2020 (⚠️ verify with ACS 5-year estimates). Zero-cost bilingual content unlocks a bloc that Nix almost certainly isn't cultivating.
- Black churches in Charlotte County + progressive mainline churches in Venice / Sarasota are over-indexed voters who turn out if they're asked; in an R+28 district, even a 5-point turnout lift on this bloc materially closes the margin.
- A paramedic has earned standing to show up in the recovery community that no other candidate will have. Natural, authentic, distinctive.

**What would falsify it:**
- No endorsement applications accepted by Labor Day despite 5+ submitted.
- Spanish-language content engagement rate below half of English-language rate (signals we're not connecting).
- Faith-community outreach producing events but no measurable registration/VBM uplift.

**How we test:**
- Month 2: submit endorsement applications to top 5 targets (see `16-endorsements.md`). Track acceptance / interview / decision dates.
- Month 3-4: pilot one Spanish post per week, measure engagement vs. English baseline.
- Month 4-6: secure 3 faith-community events; measure attendance + VBM-request pickup at each.

## Bayesian update protocol

Every 30 days:
1. List the actual data collected against each hypothesis's "how we test" plan.
2. For each hypothesis, grade: **strengthening / holding / weakening.**
3. If any hypothesis is "weakening" for two consecutive months, call a team meeting. Decide: pivot strategy, or accept the lower ceiling and redeploy effort to the hypotheses that are still holding.
4. Record the update at the top of this file with date + one-paragraph summary. Do not delete prior updates — we want the trail.

**Decision rights.** Strategy pivots require agreement between Cynthia + campaign manager. Tactical changes (swap a Tuesday event for a Thursday event, reallocate $100 from signage to printing) are the campaign manager's call.
