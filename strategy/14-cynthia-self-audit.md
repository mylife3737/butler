## Framework: disclose / contain / live with

For every flagged item, classify it:

### DISCLOSE PROACTIVELY
Items where coming forward first wins the frame:
- Old personal history that's already searchable (past financial hardship, government-assistance use — these are CORE to Cynthia's story, not liabilities).
- Prior political affiliations if they'd look like a flip without context.
- Resolved disputes / court matters already in public record.

**Playbook:** Cynthia tells the story herself, in her own voice, in a public-facing piece (email to supporters, social post, press interview). Frame it as biography — "I've been through what a lot of my neighbors are going through." Timing: early, ideally in the launch window.

### CONTAIN QUIETLY
Items that exist but are unlikely to be surfaced without deliberate opposition research:
- Minor, old, resolved matters that never made the news.
- Family-member issues that aren't Cynthia's direct issues.
- Records that exist but aren't easy to find.

**Playbook:** do NOT preemptively disclose; prepare a response in case surfaced. Response: acknowledge factually, minimize emotional weight, pivot to issue.

### LIVE WITH (accept the risk)
Items that would be damaging but are unlikely to surface and pre-disclosing would do more harm than good:
- Family-member sensitive issues (a child's medical history, etc.).
- Things that have legitimate privacy protections.

**Playbook:** document that you know about it; do not include in proactive materials; have a crisis-comms response drafted for the extreme case of surfacing.

### FATAL (rare; reassess candidacy)
Items that would make the candidacy untenable:
- Undisclosed criminal conviction involving violence or fraud.
- Pattern of behavior inconsistent with the campaign's core message (e.g., paramedic accused of patient harm).
- Financial issues that would compromise Form 6 disclosure credibility.

**Playbook:** meet with Cynthia + manager + treasurer in person (not on video). Decide: withdraw, run with full disclosure, or continue with a crisis-management plan that's probably beyond a $1K campaign's capacity. Be honest about the ceiling.

## Government-assistance history — proactive framing

Cynthia's stated bio includes "advanced her education while on government assistance." This is NOT a liability — it's a core asset IF framed well. It becomes a liability ONLY if surfaced by an opponent first.

**Recommended framing (for public content):**
> *"I put myself through nursing school on [program] while raising my kids. That's the Florida I want — one where a single mom can still do what my parents' generation did: work hard, accept help when you need it, build a life, pay it forward. My opponent voted to cut exactly the programs that got me here."*

Mamdani's "I grew up under Section 8" framing is the reference case. Don't be defensive about it; own it proudly as evidence of lived-experience competence.

**Caveat:** Cynthia must approve this framing in `INTAKE-FORM.md §10.1`. If she prefers a lighter-touch ("I was on assistance") or private ("don't lead with this"), respect that.

## Form 6 — unavoidable disclosure

Form 6 (the full financial disclosure required of Florida state legislators as of 2023) forces publication of:
- Net worth
- Assets ≥$1,000
- Liabilities ≥$1,000
- Income sources ≥$1,000

**Filed during qualifying (June 2026) and publicly searchable after.**

Anything Cynthia wouldn't want on a Florida Commission on Ethics public page cannot go on Form 6 — and Form 6 demands full disclosure. If there's a tension (e.g., a debt she'd rather not discuss), the answer is: the tension is not resolvable. Disclosure is mandatory. Prepare emotionally; prepare a message for any line items that might draw attention.

**Recommended:** Cynthia works with a local accountant or volunteer CPA to complete Form 6 carefully. One rushed form = a correction later that becomes a news story.
